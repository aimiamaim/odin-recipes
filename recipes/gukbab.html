Busan Dwaeiji Gukbap

Nam Yejun's favourite food

The hot, flavouful, rich and aromatic soup of Busan Dwaeji Gukbap is the best served with a hot bowl of rice. Adding a small amount of salted baby shrimps will bring even richer flavour to it. Gukbap or soup and rice has a variety of main ingredients, here we will focus on Busan style pork soup.

Here is the recipe from Travel Food Atlas

Ingredients

  • Pork shank and/or knuckle bones (ok if some meat is still on them) - 5 lbs.
  • 1-2 lbs pork belly or butt (optional if bones have meat on them) - 2 lbs.
  • Ginger (3 inch piece) - 1
  • White onion (cut in half) - 1
  • Garlic cloves - 4
  • Steamed Rice - 4 cups
  • Green onions (sliced) - 4
  • Chinese chives (chopped) - 4
  • Gochujang - to taste

Instructions

aaa
  1. Add the pork bones and pork belly/butt to a large stock pot and cover with water.
  2. Place over high heat and bring to a boil, then lower heat to a rolling simmer for 25 minutes.
  3. Drain bones into colander and toss all the liquid.
  4. Wash out the stock pot and rinse the bones and meat so no scum remains.
  5. Add the bones then the meat back to the now clean pot and cover with water (about an extra inch) again.
  6. Bring to a just boil, then lower to a simmer for 45 mins to an 1 hour.
  7. When the meat is tender but not falling apart, remove the pork belly/butt and any bones with meat on them.
  8. Add them to a large bowl of ice water, to lower temperature and prevent discoloration.
  9. When cool enough to handle, remove the meat from the bones, and the belly/butt, pat the meat dry and store in the fridge until the soup is ready.
  10. Drain out half of the stock in the pot.
  11. Add the removed bones back to the pot and again add enough water to cover.
  12. Bring to just a boil, then to a low simmer for 8-12 hrs.
  13. Once your stock has simmered for at least 8 hrs, remove meat from the fridge and slice into thin slabs.
  14. Allow the meat to come to room temperature.
  15. Bring the broth to a boil.
  16. The broth should be free of any debris. Strain through a mesh sieve or cheesecloth to have a clear broth and set aside.
  17. Rinse the pot once again, add clear broth back to the pot and then return to a boil.
  18. Add 1 cup of rice into each serving bowl, cover with boiling broth and season with garnishes to taste.
